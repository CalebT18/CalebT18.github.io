You are running through the woods, your feet pounding against the dirt, twigs snapping beneath you like bones cracking in the silence. You wear floppy bunny ears and grip a half-eaten carrot—part of the game, part of the mockery. The darkness around you feels alive, thick with the tension of the hunt. The trees blur past, their shadows stretching like claws.

You don’t know how long you’ve been running, only that you’re not ready to stop. Then, up ahead, the path splits in two. On the left: a narrow cave entrance, barely visible but promising shelter. On the right: a hooded stranger standing in a small clearing, hand outstretched, voice calm. “I can help you.”

What do you say?
